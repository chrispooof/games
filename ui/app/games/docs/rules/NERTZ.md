# Nertz Game Rules

## Dealing

- Each player has a deck of 52 cards that are different from each other
- Each player makes a pile of 13 cards called the Nertz pile, with the top card visible
- Next to the pile each player deals four cards face up side by side and not overlapping that are work piles.
- The remaining cards are held face-down in a stock pile. They will be turned 3 at a time onto a face stack, forming the player's waste pile.
- Players arrange themselves around a common area that all players can access easily.
- The common area is where the foundations will be placed and built on.

## Gameplay

- Players play simultaneously no taking turns around their own table.
- A players main goal is to eliminate all cards from their Nertz pile.
- A player who has eliminated all their Nertz cards may call "Nertz!" at which point the game immediately ends. Cards that were already in play can finish their last move, but no new moves can be made.
- The player who finishes their Nertz pile can call "Nertz!" at any point they don't have to do it immediately.
- If two or more players try to play to the same foundation at the same time, the first played card stays there and all other players have to take their card back to their stock or work pile.

## Work Piles

- A player's four work piles begin with one card each.
- Work piles are built in descending order, alternating color, overlapping the cards.
- Example: A red six is placed on a black seven, a black five on a red six, etc.
- You can move any work pile card to another of your own work piles.
- When a space results, it may be filled by a card from your nertz pile, your waste pile, or another work pile.
- The exposed card of each work pile, (i.e. the lowest ranked cards of each pile) are available to be played onto the foundations.
- You can also move cards that are higher ranked than the bottom of your work pile. Example: If the bottom card of a work pile is a red six, you can move a black seven to be behind the six.

## Nertz Pile

- Cards at the top of your nertz pile can be played in empty work piles, onto one of your existing work piles, or directly onto a foundation.
- When your nertz pile is empty you are entitled to call "Nertz!", but you don't have to immediately.

## Foundations

- Foundations are built in the common area.
- They are **always** begun with an ace, and can be built up by playing the next highest card of the **same** suit until the king is reached.
- Players can always start a new foundation by placing any available ace in the common area.
- Other available cards can be played on top of any existing foundation, given another player doesn't get there before you.
- You can use cards from your Nertz pile, exposed cards from your work piles, or the top card from your waste pile to build foundations.
- When the king is placed on a foundation, that foundation is complete, turned over, and can no longer be used.

## Stock and Waste Piles

- You can turn over cards from your stock three at a time and put them face-up onto your waste pile (the waste pile has no cards at the start of play).
- Keep cards in the same order when moving them from stock to waste.
- The top card of the waste pile is always available to be played onto a foundation or work pile.
- If you have one or two cards left in your stock pile, you can take one or two to make three from the bottom of the waste pile and then start over.

## Scoring

- When someone calls Nertz, play ends and scoring takes place.
- Each plater gets one point for each of their own cards that they managed to play into the common area.
- To determine this the foundation piles need to be sorted out according to the owners of the cards, hence why you need different cards for each deck.
- All players except the one who called Nertz then subtract two points for every card left in their Nertz pile when play ended.
- In the case that all players are so stuck that no card in their stock will play, play stops and the score is calculated as usual, where each player subtracts 2 points for every card left in their Nertz pile.
- Further deals are played until one or more players' scores reach or exceed an agreed target score (usually 100).
